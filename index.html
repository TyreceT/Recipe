Lumpiang Shanghai



Ingredients:
  • 500g ground pork
  • 1 medium carrot, grated
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 20 pcs spring roll wrappers
  • Cooking oil for frying
Procedure:
  1. In a large bowl, mix ground pork, grated carrot, chopped onion, minced garlic, egg, salt, and black pepper. Use your hands to combine everything well.
  2. Lay a spring roll wrapper on a flat surface. Place a spoonful of the pork mixture near the bottom, then roll it tightly, folding in the sides. Seal the edge with a dab of water.
  3. Heat oil in a deep pan. To check if it's hot enough, drop a small piece of wrapper—if it bubbles, it's ready.
  4. Fry lumpia in batches, turning occasionally until golden brown and crispy.
  5. Remove from oil and drain on paper towels. Serve hot with sweet chilli sauce.

Pancit Bihon



Ingredients:
  • 250g bihon noodles
  • 1/2 kg chicken breast, boiled and shredded
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 1/4 small cabbage, shredded
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • Cooking oil
Procedure:
  1. Soak bihon noodles in water until soft. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a kawali. Sauté garlic and onion until fragrant.
  3. Add shredded chicken and stir-fry for a few minutes.
  4. Pour in soy sauce and chicken broth. Bring to a gentle simmer.
  5. Add carrots and cabbage, stirring well.
  6. Place the softened bihon noodles into the kawali, gently mixing everything together. Stir continuously until the noodles absorb the broth and soften.
  7. Season with salt and pepper. Taste and adjust if needed.
  8. Serve warm with calamansi on the side.

Cassava Cake



Ingredients:
  • 2 cups grated cassava
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup condensed milk
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 2 tbsp melted butter
  • 1/4 cup grated cheese (for topping)
  • Banana leaves (optional for lining)
Procedure:
  1. In a large bowl, combine grated cassava, coconut milk, condensed milk, beaten eggs, and melted butter. Stir until well mixed.
  2. Grease a llanera or baking pan with butter or line it with banana leaves.
  3. Pour the cassava mixture into the pan and spread evenly.
  4. Steam the cassava cake over medium heat for about 1 hour or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  5. Once cooked, sprinkle grated cheese on top and let it melt from the residual heat. If preferred, use a torch to lightly toast the cheese.
  6. Allow to cool slightly before slicing and serving.
